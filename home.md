# Hi, I'm Zack Smith

I write about privacy, digital sovereignty, and the tools we build to stay free online.

## What you'll find here

This is a small corner of the internet where I publish essays and projects on:

* **Privacy** — encryption, surveillance, and the quiet erosion of the default-private web.
* **Anti-censorship** — circumvention tools, mesh networks, and resisting single points of control.
* **Sovereignty** — owning your data, your identity, and your infrastructure instead of renting it.

## Essays
 
I write because most people never get to see how the systems around them actually work — and what that not-knowing costs them. These essays are my attempt to make the invisible visible: the tradeoffs baked into the tools we use, the quiet defaults that shape what "normal" means online.

## Projects
 
I build small, focused tools rather than platforms — things that do one job well and don't ask for more trust than they need. Most of what I make sits somewhere between privacy tooling and anti-censorship infrastructure: encrypted messaging, circumvention, self-hosting, and software that puts control back in the hands of the person running it.
